SMART FAB INSOLE

I answered to the above questions and I make a first prototype of the smart insole.

What will it do?

This project is a reactive insole that uses a custom textile pressure sensor to measure and visualize the pressure of the feet. This sole is like a device to interact with another output device (light, speaker etc.). For this reason I will created a web application that wirelessly tracks data from the insole , via bluetooth, and provides a variety of instantaneous feedback. Users can choose from visual, audio or sensory feedback.

The insole should:

measure the pressure of the feet
interact through light (optional)
interact through sound (optional)

Who’s done what beforehand?

I found a lot of commercial project about smart insole that also implement electronic interactions and web application too. All projects references to a tipology of shoes like sneakers and not for high heel.

There’s a kickstarter project: “Digitsole” , Digitsole is a connected insole on the market controlled via your smartphone - warm your feet, track your distance and calories (https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/1308642275/digitsole-the-first-interactive-insole-to-heat-you) Thermacell is a battery-powered heated insoles (http://www.mackspw.com/Item–i-THRHS1S) Another project was developed by the Utah University : Rapid Rehab system, will eventually help correct walking problems for people with artificial legs, hip replacements and broken legs.

What materials and components will be required?

silicon for the insole
wood panel for the mold
plastic filament and/ or resin for the case of the bluetooth module
plastic filament and/or wood for the shoe that content the insole

Electronic, most important components:

ATtiny44
analog pressure sensor textile kit (velostat and conductive fabric)
bluetooth module
leds pendant (custom leds board) 
and then: resistors, capacitors, connectors.

Where will they come from?

The heart of the sensor is the custom textile sensor, that is composed from two parts: velostat and conductive fabric. Velostat is a piezoresistive material. These materials come from Plug and wear, a company expertise in textile and textile machinery and actually in electronic textile. All products are designed and manifactured in Florence(Italy). About the electronics most of the device are available at FabLab and I got the rest from online shopping.

How much will it cost?

The most expensive part is the silicon (about 20-30 euro) and about another 8 euro for a wood panel. Between the electronic components the less chipper is the press sensor textile kit about 12 euro. I think another 10 euro to produce the board with the bluetooth module (hc5-05). It’s not easy to evaluate the real cost, but I think that his total cost is about 50 euro.

What parts and systems will be made?

I’m going to make the mold of the insole with the pressure sensor inside. I ‘ll design the board with the bluetooth module to receive the sensor data in real time. This board ‘ll be wearable and content in a 3d printed case with a special clip to connect to the shoe.To visualize the data I thinked to write a web application . 

smart insole + shoe case

led pendant
